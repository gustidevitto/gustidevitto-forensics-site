## üéØ **PSYCHOLOGICAL COPYWRITING TECHNIQUES APPLIED**

### **1. Visceral Opening Scenarios**
- Specific dates and times (e.g., "Monday, 09:14")
- Exact amounts (e.g., "Rp187 juta shortfall")
- Emotional triggers (e.g., "payroll crisis countdown")

### **2. Quantified Statistics**
- Audit sample sizes (247-687 businesses)
- Specific percentages (e.g., "79% have <30 days runway")
- Timeline metrics (e.g., "11 days from safe to crisis")
- Loss amounts (e.g., "Rp564M/year")

### **3. Surgical Language**
- Medical metaphors: "pendarahan," "kolaps," "oxygen"
- Forensic terms: "diagnosis," "intervention," "autopsy"
- Military precision: "target," "strike," "eliminate"

### **4. Villain Redefinition**
- Attacking structural issues, not people
- Examples: "silent bleeding," "dead capital," "phantom costs"
- Shifting blame from individuals to systems

### **5. Outcome-Focused Framing**
- Before/after metrics (e.g., "survival rate: 31% ‚Üí 86%")
- Intervention success rates (e.g., "+420% velocity increase")
- Financial impact (e.g., "Rp840M annual leakage recovered")

### **6. Urgency and Fear of Loss**
- Time-sensitive scenarios (e.g., "67 days of runway")
- Cost of inaction (e.g., "3.2 months to cash crisis")
- Countdown timers (e.g., "Cash-Zero Date: April 19, 2026")

### **7. Brutal Closing Questions**
- Direct challenges (e.g., "Are you growing or dying slowly?")
- Self-examination prompts (e.g., "Can you survive 9 days without cash?")
- Reality checks (e.g., "Is this a business or just paying costs?")
