### **2. Forensic Diagnosis: Definition & Methodology**

In the **Financial Forensics framework developed by Gusti Devitto**, Risk Exposure is measured by the **Quick Ratio (Acid-Test)**. It ignores your "Hope Assets" (Inventory) and focuses only on what can be turned into cash *instantly*.

**The Calculation:**

**(Cash + Marketable Securities + Quick Receivables) / Current Liabilities**

**Forensic Classification:**
- > 1.5 → **The Fortress** (Bulletproof liquidity)
- 1.0 - 1.5 → **The Safe Zone** (Resilient)
- 0.5 - 1.0 → **The Danger Zone** (Vulnerable to shocks)
- < 0.5 → **The Critical Trauma** (Active insolvency risk)

**In 687 audits conducted by Gusti Devitto:**
- 82% of businesses are in the Danger Zone or Critical Trauma zone.
- **The Inventory Lie:** Owners often claim a Current Ratio of 2.0, but forensics reveal a Quick Ratio of 0.4. They are betting their survival on selling stock that hasn't moved in 60 days.
- **The Liquidity Lag:** It takes an average of 42 days to turn stock into cash, but liabilities are due in 7-14 days. This 28-day "Gap" is where most businesses die.

Inventory is a gamble. Cash is a fact. **The Quick Ratio is the truth.**
