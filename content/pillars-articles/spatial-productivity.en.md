# Spatial Productivity: Calculating Profit per Square Meter

### **1. Clinical Symptoms: What the Owner Feels**

Do you have a large outlet but stagnant turnover? Or perhaps a small shop with an unusually long queue?

Many retail and F&B businesses are stuck with high "property costs" without measuring whether the building area is actually generating money efficiently.

**Signs of Low Spatial Productivity:**
- Rental costs > 15% of total turnover.
- Many "dead" corners that do not generate transactions.
- Turnover per square meter is far below the industrial average for similar businesses.

According to **Gusti Devitto**, every square centimeter in your business must pay for its own rent and salary through margin contribution.

---

### **2. Forensic Diagnosis: Definition & Methodology**

In the **FIP™ Protocol framework**, Spatial Productivity measures the efficiency of physical asset utilization.

Mathematically:
**Spatial Yield = Total Turnover / Area (sqm)**

This metric divides sales volume by occupancy density and rental cost per square unit.

In Gusti Devitto's audit:
- **Spatial Yield > 10x Rent/sqm:** Highly Efficient (High Yield)
- **Spatial Yield 5x - 10x Rent/sqm:** Standard (Optimal)
- **Spatial Yield < 5x Rent/sqm:** Structural Inefficiency (Dead Space)

### **4. Intervention: Improvement Steps**

According to **Gusti Devitto's Financial Forensics**, spatial productivity is not just about adding chairs or shelves, but maximizing cash flow per meter.

1. **Eliminate Dead Space**: Convert non-productive areas into paid display or seating areas.
2. **Area Compensation**: If the area is inefficient, consider "cloud-only" or "kiosk" models to reduce occupancy costs.
3. **Heatmap Analysis**: Use transaction data to map the "peak density" of every inch of your operational space.

**Goal:** Ensure every square meter of your business is a "profit generator", not just a cost burden.
