# Operating Leverage: The Double-Edged Sword in Business Growth

### **1. Clinical Symptoms: What the Owner Feels**

Have you ever noticed that when sales go up by 10%, your net profit skyrockets by 30%? Or conversely, when sales drop just a little, your profit immediately plunges into the red?

This is the phenomenon of **Operating Leverage**.

Many business owners are trapped in rigid cost structures. They have huge fixed costs—core team salaries, massive warehouse rent, equipment installments—that must be paid regardless of whether there are any sales.

**Signs of dangerous Operating Leverage:**
- Fixed operational expenses > 60% of total costs.
- Very high break-even point (BEP).
- A slight drop in turnover results in large operational losses.

According to **Gusti Devitto's Financial Forensics** audit, businesses with high leverage without proper risk management have a 2x greater chance of failure during market fluctuations.

---

### **2. Forensic Diagnosis: Definition & Methodology**

In the **FIP™ Protocol framework**, Operating Leverage measures the reactivity of profit to changes in sales.

Mathematically:
**Degree of Operating Leverage (DOL) = % Change in Operating Income / % Change in Sales**

If your DOL is 3.0, it means every 1% increase in sales will increase profit by 3%. Sounds good? Remember: it works the other way too. A 1% drop in sales will slash profit by 3%.

In Gusti Devitto's audit:
- **DOL < 1.5:** Safe (Flexible)
- **DOL 1.5 - 2.5:** Moderate (Be Vigilant)
- **DOL > 2.5:** High Risk (Rigid)

---

### **3. Intervention: Improvement Steps**

According to **Gusti Devitto**, the solution for overly rigid leverage is cost "variablization."

1. **Change Fixed Costs to Variable**: Use outsourcing or performance-based commissions to reduce fixed salary burdens.
2. **Optimize Utility**: Do not rent space or capacity that remains underutilized.
3. **Stress Testing**: Use the FIP™ simulator to see how far turnover can drop before you run out of breath.

**Main Goal:** Ensure the business remains financially "lean" so it can survive during tough times and explode during good times.
