# Data Integrity Score: When Numbers Become Structured Lies

### **1. Clinical Symptoms: What the Owner Feels**

Have you ever noticed your financial reports looking "too tidy" or perhaps constantly "disconnected" from the physical inventory and the bank balance?

The Data Integrity Score is the key metric to detect whether the data you receive from the operational team, cashiers, or admin is HONEST or MANIPULATED data.

**Signs of Non-Integrity Data:**
- Sales figures consistently differ from POS receipts.
- System inventory reports always differ from manual stocktakes.
- Operational data (e.g., working hours) is logically inconsistent with the actual output.

According to **Gusti Devitto**, figures that look good on paper but bad in the bank are symptoms of systemic manipulation.

---

### **2. Forensic Diagnosis: Definition & Methodology**

In the **FIP™ Protocol framework**, the Data Integrity Score measures the consistency and mathematical reasonableness of the data points entered.

We use a series of logic tests, such as **Benford's Law** and cross-pillar correlation (e.g., does the increase in turnover correlate with a rational increase in COGS?).

In Gusti Devitto's audit:
- **Score > 90:** Highly Reliable
- **Score 75 - 90:** Requires Periodic Verification
- **Score < 75:** Serious Potential Fraud or Systemic Error

### **4. Intervention: Improvement Steps**

According to **Gusti Devitto's Financial Forensics**, data integrity is not about punishing people, but about building systems that are antithetical to dishonesty.

1. **Automatic Synchronization**: Eliminate as much manual input as possible. Use API integrations between systems.
2. **Double-Blind Audit**: Perform cross-audits between different teams unexpectedly.
3. **Forensic Pillars**: Use the FIP™ Protocol to automatically detect data anomalies across 25 different points.

**The Goal:** Ensure you as the owner make decisions based on **FACTS**, not the fiction created by your team.
